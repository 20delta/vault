## ILRLE 3486, Field Experiments in Economics and Business

#### 9/17-19, Experimental Designs and Statistics

##### Story: 
In ==designing experiments==, you have ==important decisions== to make before you can ==collect your outcomes==. As you do this you use ==statistics== to make sense of the results where ==intuition== is important.

##### Key Notes:

P Values indicate the likelihood we would observe a value that is at least as extreme as the one observed if the null hypothesis we're true
###### Design Decisions
What decisions are involved in designing an experiment?

The control group should show you what would happen if treatments didn't occur.

You want to be able to create a causal argument from the inclusion of a treatment.

There are two encouraged types:
* Encouragement Design: Provide incentive to encourage uptake of existent intervention
* Information Provision: Provide information to prompt the uptake of certain beliefs
Others Include:
* Audit Design:  Implement an intervention to see changes in behavior.
You can mix and match design types. It is very discretionary.

Real effort tasks are great for experiments because they show intent well

###### Collecting Outcomes

Outcomes collected should be measured in ways that answer the hypothesis

Outcomes should be specific and measurable. Their quality depends on the information content of answer, degree of measurement error and more.

Serveral Types of Outcomes:
* Reviewed Outcomes,
* Stated Choices
* Observed Choice

Reviewed outcomes are prefereded by economist (similiar to real effort tasks)
