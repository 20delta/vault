## NBA 3000, Creating New Ventures

#### 8/27, Introduction
Entrepreneurship is the pursuit of an activity where you don't have all the resources; it asks "what resources do I need? Is it worth it?"

A start up is a temporary organization that is searching for a repeatable scalable business.

###### Entrepreneurial Process
1. Market Identification
	* Two main processes:
		* Finding a problem your solution you can solve
		* Find an opportunity you can address
2. Business Model Design
	* Articulate a business idea -> identify key assumptions -> do interviews as a lo-fi test
3. Proper Testing
	* Do MVP Tests. These don't have to be with a semi-complete product. They can be for only a part of the process, or for just one assumption. You can have something that is like a sign up sheet where people give you their email to express interest in a product idea.
	* For areas/industries of high uncertainty, do tests that cost little time and money

A good entrepreneur is one that earnestly masters the process. They prove themselves wrong and honestly learn the process.

#### 8/29, Opportunities
The customer only cares that your product solves an issue; Let your product create value for customers as it an addresses an opportunity.
##### Opportunities
Opportunities exists in two way: within value chains or for disruption

Value chains are opportunities those within chains of activities, where something can can be changed to create value for someone participating in the chain. Value chain mapping--mapping out the steps within the chain--is a valuable tool for finding opportunities here.
	- Value chain mapping
		1. Identify a process or workflow
		2. Interview individuals
		3. find pain points
		4. identify benefits/performance they need in a solution

##### Key Observations
An important issue is framing the problem

Its not smart or productive to act on "I'm making a product for x" without supporting data because this means you don't actually have a customer, or "I am the customer" because its easy to act from biases.

Once you have the problem framed you can go to the solution space; Problem framing -> Solution Space. In this your framing of the problem will affect the solution you come up with.
Ex.
Elevator Slow  -> Make Elevator faster
People don't like the wait -> Make the wait feel shorter

You can identify a high value problem by thinking about how much effort is being put into solving it.

Start ups usually fail by not focusing on performance that's important to customer (they are focused on the benefits that appeal the most to them), not moving to a new S-Curve, rushing to exploit a perceived opportunity.
